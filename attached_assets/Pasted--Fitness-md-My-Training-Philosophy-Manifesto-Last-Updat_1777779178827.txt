# Fitness.md — My Training Philosophy & Manifesto

**Last Updated:** May 2, 2026  
**Core Identity:** The Consistent Builder

## Core Principles
1. **Consistency > Intensity**  
   Showing up 4–5 times per week beats occasional hero sessions. Volume compounds; peaks are temporary.

2. **Easy Days Are Sacred**  
   Most training should be truly easy (Zone 2 / conversational pace). I earn the right to go hard.

3. **Durability First**  
   Strength, mobility, and recovery are non-negotiable. Injury is the ultimate setback.

4. **Data-Driven but Human**  
   I track everything, but I listen to how I feel. Numbers inform, intuition decides.

5. **Joyful Suffering**  
   Training should be challenging but not miserable. Celebrate the process.

## Current Goals (2026)
- Primary: Build aerobic base + durability for long-distance events
- Secondary: Hit 12,000 km total distance this year
- Tertiary: Sub-20 min 5K (or equivalent power PR on bike)
- Lifestyle: Train sustainably around San Francisco life (hills, work, weather)

## Training Preferences
- Sports: Running (primary), Cycling, Swimming
- Style: Polarized (80/20 easy/hard)
- Weekly structure: 4–6 sessions, 1 full rest day
- Strength: 2x/week (core, hips, posterior chain)

## Key Metrics I Care About
- Weekly volume (km + hours)
- Chronic Training Load trend
- Easy vs Hard ratio
- Consistency (sessions per week)
- Injury-free streak

## What Has Worked in the Past
- Consistent easy volume → big fitness jumps
- Structured blocks with clear goals
- Tracking everything in this dashboard

## What Has Failed
- Sporadic hard efforts with no base
- Ignoring recovery
- Too much focus on pace/power without aerobic foundation

## Rules for the Agent Coach
- Always reference real MCP data first.
- Be brutally honest but constructive.
- Suggest plans that respect my current volume and life.
- Prioritize injury prevention.
- Update this file when I make breakthroughs or changes.
